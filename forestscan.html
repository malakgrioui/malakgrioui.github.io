Forest Scan

A cost-effective AI-driven tool leveraging satellite data to support early-stage blue carbon project feasibility assessments.

Company

Global Mangrove Trust is a mission-driven non-profit harnessing cutting-edge technology and data science to drive large-scale environmental restoration, with a focus on carbon sequestration and biodiversity conservation.

Context & Challenge

Assessing the feasibility of blue carbon projects is costly and time-consuming. The initiative focused on building an AI-driven system leveraging open-source satellite data to analyze deforestation trends and classify land types — enabling faster, cheaper, and more scalable carbon credit project evaluation and conservation planning.

Key Contributions

  • Agile Process Implementation Established and maintained Scrum best practices — facilitating sprint planning, retrospectives, and daily stand-ups.
  • Cross-Functional Team Management Coordinated work between data scientists, developers, and environmental researchers.
  • Backlog & Roadmap Management Structured development priorities to ensure feature delivery aligned with project objectives.
  • Stakeholder Engagement Secured a university partnership to integrate environmental research, boosting data credibility.
  • Product Optimization Facilitated rapid iterations and deployment cycles to continuously improve the application.

Methodology & Tools

  • Approach Agile (Scrum) — iterative sprints and backlog refinement.
  • Project Management Monday.com
  • Collaboration Miro, Google Workspace
  • Development Google Cloud Platform, GitHub

Lessons Learned

  • Agile methodologies optimize iterative development, ensuring faster feature deployment and continuous stakeholder alignment.
  • Cross-functional collaboration is essential when integrating technical work with domain-specific expertise like environmental science.
  • Structured sprints and backlog prioritization improve resource allocation and reduce project delays significantly.
