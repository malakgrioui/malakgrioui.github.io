Forest Scan

A cost-effective AI-driven tool leveraging satellite data to support early-stage blue carbon project feasibility assessments.

Company

Global Mangrove Trust – A mission-driven non-profit harnessing cutting-edge technology and data science to drive large-scale environmental restoration, focusing on carbon sequestration and biodiversity conservation.

Context

The initiative focused on building an AI-driven system leveraging open-source satellite data to analyze deforestation trends and classify land types, aiding carbon credit projects and conservation efforts.

Key Contributions

  • Agile Process Implementation: Established and maintained Scrum best practices, facilitating sprint planning, retrospectives, and daily stand-ups.
  • Cross-Functional Team Management: Coordinated work between data scientists and developers.
  • Backlog & Roadmap Management: Structured development priorities and ensured feature delivery aligned with project objectives.
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Engaged stakeholders and secured a university partnership to integrate environmental research, boosting data credibility.
  • Product Optimization: Facilitated rapid iterations and deployment to improve the App.

Methodology & Tools

  • Approach: Agile (Scrum) – Implemented iterative sprints and backlog refinement.

  • Tools Used:
    • Project Management: Monday
    • Collaboration: Miro, Google Workspace
    • Development: Google Cloud Platform, Github

Lessons Learned

  • Agile methodologies optimize iterative development, ensuring faster feature deployment and stakeholder alignment.
  • Cross-functional collaboration is essential for integrating technical and environmental expertise.
  • Structured sprints and backlog prioritization improve resource allocation, minimizing project delays.
