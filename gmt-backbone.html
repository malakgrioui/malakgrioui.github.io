GMT Delivery Backbone

Design and implementation of GMT's Retooling — a project management and reporting system that transforms scattered work plans, budgets, partner reports, and impact data into one structured delivery backbone for conservation projects.

Organisation

Global Mangrove Trust is a mission-driven non-profit using technology and data science to drive large-scale environmental restoration. As the organisation scaled its operations across multiple geographies and partners, it needed a reliable, structured system to make project delivery visible, accountable, and easy to manage.

Context & Challenge

GMT faced growing operational complexity: fragmented reporting across partners, backlog accumulation, and insufficient accountability toward investors, communities, and registry partners. Work plans, budgets, quality checks, and impact data existed in silos with no clear thread between them. The challenge was to design a coherent delivery backbone — one that could track every activity from planning through evidence submission, QC review, and impact reporting, and that would give teams and stakeholders a shared, reliable view of what was delivered and what was not.

Key Contributions

  • Operational Model Design Structured GMT's delivery operations around standardized Work Units — trackable activity blocks connecting planned work, budgets, partner reports, and impact data.
  • Quarterly Delivery Cycle Designed the end-to-end cycle: defining work orders, collecting partner progress reports, running QC checks, updating impact data, and routing unfinished work into a structured backlog for the next quarter.
  • Quality Control Process Built a QC framework to distinguish between delivered, incomplete, and backlog work — with clear evidence requirements and review checkpoints at each stage.
  • Airtable Data Architecture Designed the Airtable-based data structure to track work units, partner submissions, delivery status, evidence links, and budget use in one coherent system.
  • Partner Reporting Adaptation Co-designed structured reporting templates with field partners (e.g. Yagasu quarterly prototype), capturing activities by category, units, participant data, village details, and evidence links.
  • Documentation & Onboarding Produced process documentation and workflow diagrams to support team adoption and stakeholder communication.

Methodology & Tools

  • Approach Iterative design with continuous stakeholder validation — from operational mapping to prototype testing with field teams.
  • Data Architecture Airtable
  • Field Data Collection KoboToolbox
  • Diagramming & Process Design Miro, Figma
  • Collaboration Google Workspace

Lessons Learned

  • Accountability in field operations starts with a clear, shared definition of what "delivered" means — without that, every system built on top will reflect the same ambiguity.
  • Structured backlog logic is not a failure mechanism — it's a quality mechanism. Moving unfinished work forward with visibility is far more useful than discarding it or hiding it in narrative reports.
  • Adapting partner-facing tools to local field realities is as important as designing the internal system — if the reporting side is too complex, the whole delivery loop breaks down at the source.
