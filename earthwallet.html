EarthWallet

A B2B2C platform enabling banks to integrate sustainability into their financial products through eco-friendly payment cards.

Company

Handprint.Tech is a tech startup focused on embedding sustainability into everyday transactions — making it simple for companies and consumers to take meaningful environmental action at scale.

Context & Challenge

The goal was to develop a scalable financial product enabling banks to offer eco-friendly payment cards — empowering millions of consumers to fund environmental restoration projects with every transaction. The project required a robust product strategy, a seamless UX, and strong technical foundations for global scalability.

Key Contributions

  • Product Roadmap Defined and executed a structured roadmap aligning with business goals and evolving market needs.
  • User & Market Research Conducted competitive analysis and user interviews to validate product-market fit.
  • Functional Specifications Created detailed user stories, requirements, and system diagrams to guide development.
  • Cross-functional Coordination Managed collaboration between engineering, design, and business teams for timely, high-quality delivery.
  • API & Data Architecture Contributed to defining data architecture and API integrations for seamless transactions.
  • Stakeholder Communication Aligned with banking partners and sustainability organizations on product features and constraints.
  • Testing & QA Oversaw product testing phases to ensure high standards for usability and performance.

Methodology & Tools

  • Approach Agile (Scrum) — iterative sprints with continuous user feedback.
  • Project Management Jira, Azure DevOps
  • Design & Prototyping Figma, Miro
  • API Testing Postman
  • Collaboration Slack, Notion, Google Workspace

Lessons Learned

  • Aligning tech with sustainability goals requires deep collaboration between financial institutions, regulators, and tech teams — assumptions cost time.
  • Iterative development and rapid feedback loops significantly enhance product-market fit, allowing quick pivots before costly mistakes.
  • Cross-functional communication is the invisible foundation of execution — bridging engineering, design, and business prevents rework and delays.
