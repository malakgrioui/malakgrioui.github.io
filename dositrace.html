Dositrace 4.0

Next-generation dose management software for medical imaging — upgraded UX/UI, streamlined workflows, and enhanced device interoperability.

Company

Biomediqa is a leader in radiation protection, medical physics, and internal quality control — providing innovative solutions for medical imaging, research, and compliance to ensure patient safety and operational excellence.

Context & Challenge

The project focused on a comprehensive upgrade of Dositrace, enhancing its UX/UI for a more intuitive clinical experience, streamlining workflow automation to reduce manual tasks for radiologists and technicians, and integrating advanced data interoperability for seamless communication between imaging devices and hospital information systems.

Key Contributions

  • Product Roadmap & Strategy Defined and executed the Dositrace 4.0 roadmap incorporating user feedback, compliance updates, and technical constraints.
  • Cross-functional Coordination Managed collaboration between developers, healthcare professionals, and QA teams across the release cycle.
  • UX/UI Design Created user-friendly wireframes and interactive prototypes to improve interface intuitiveness for clinical users.
  • Process Optimization Redesigned user workflows, improving efficiency in ionizing dose management and reporting.
  • Medical Device Integration Ensured seamless connection of Dositrace with over 20 medical imaging devices via HL7 protocols.

Methodology & Tools

  • Approach Agile (Scrum) — iterative sprints and backlog refinement.
  • UX/UI Design Figma
  • Project Management Miro
  • Collaboration Microsoft Workspace
  • Integration Linux, SQL, HL7

Lessons Learned

  • UX/UI improvements are the highest-leverage driver of software adoption — especially in regulated environments where users can't simply switch tools.
  • Interdisciplinary collaboration between healthcare professionals and software teams is not optional; the domain knowledge gap requires active bridging.
  • Medical data integration demands rigorous validation before and after deployment — patient safety stakes leave no room for assumption.
